Machine Learning and Critical Technical Practice

For the next several week we will learn about the mathematical and technical aspects of machine learning while engaging in something called critical technical practice

21

Example Excellent Thesis from last year

While Instagram presents itself as a platform for creativity and connection— designed to connect friends and even strangers— its increasing reliance on AI, particularly its recommendation algorithm, reveals a deeper priority: maximizing engagement, and therefore profit, at the expense of authentic user experience and control. By using machine learning models to predict and influence what users see, Instagram blurs the lines between personalization and control. If Instagram were made for and by college students, the algorithm would shift to be more student-centered and ethical by prioritizing transparency, equity, and user agency over profit-driven engagement.

22

Another example thesis

Introducing virtual assistants like Bank of America’s “Erica” helps its users effectively manage their money and learn how to make smarter financial decisions. By fully understanding the capabilities of these technologies while acknowledging and addressing various concerns that may arise, AI personal finance assistants can improve how we learn about financial literacy and change the future of banking.

Critical Technical Practice is a term developed by Phil Agre to describe a way of approaching technological work

25

A critical technical practice will, at least for the foreseeable future, require a split identity -- one foot planted in the craft work of design and the other foot planted in the reflexive work of critique.

26

Successfully spanning these borderlands, bridging the disparate sites of practice that computer work brings uncomfortably together, will require a historical understanding of the institutions and methods of the field, and it will draw on this understanding as a resource in choosing problems, evaluating solutions, diagnosing difficulties, and motivating alternative proposals. (Agre, 1998)
