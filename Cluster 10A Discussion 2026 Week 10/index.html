High Level Feedback

  • Great research and use of sources
  • Very interesting and creative arguments
  • A lot of good ideas in the conclusion either presenting an alternative dataset, a new way of thinking about and collecting data, or some other intervention
6

Room for Improvement

  • Don't forget to ground your analysis in missing data
  • Make the missing data aspect of the problem you are analyzing clear from the introduction and thesis statement
  • Make sure you are referencing and citing Mimi Onuoha in your discussion of missing data
  • Make sure you include your AI attestation sheet
7

Thesis Statement Common Mistakes

  • Thesis is presented without context or sentences to set up and support
  • Argument or keypoint is unclear
  • Significance is not communicated
  • Thesis feels disconnected
  • Problem that the thesis is addressing is not clear

Citations and Use of Evidence

  • Unclear how citation connects or supports argument
  • Citation formatted incorrectly
  • Quotation presented without context or connection back to key point or claim
11

Works Cited/References Page

  • Works cited page should not have bullets or numbers
  • Entries should be alphabetized by the first item in the citation, usually the authors last name
  • Works Cited and references should be on a separate page, not the same page as your conclusion
  • Check example papers formatted using your citation style and compare with your paper. You can find these on the Purdue OWL website

Peer Review

Open the peer review assignments Google sheet and Peer review worksheet on the course site

  1. Open the tab with your section
  2. Make a copy of the peer review worksheet linked on the course site. After making a copy share it with ngozih@g.ucla.edu and with both peers. Add both peer names to the title and to the top of the document
  3. Add the link to your peer review worksheet to your row on the google sheet
  4. Next each peer should add the link to a google doc with your rough draft to the google sheet. Make sure you share it with the other peer and with ngozih@g.ucla.edu
  5. Read through the rough draft of your peer, fill out the peer review worksheet and discuss feedback
