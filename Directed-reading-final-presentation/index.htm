Research Statement

My research primarily focuses on critical engagement with artificial intelligence as a philosophical concept, technological artifact, and tool for accessing information and producing knowledge. I seek to launch a critique of the project of Artificial Intelligence as a concretization of enlightenment reason and racial knowledge primarily through a critical engagement with logic and information.

I define information as loosely as semantic content concretized in material objects. However much of the power of information as a concept is its ontological fluidity and ability to change form in different sociot-technical contexts. Logic refers to formal methods of reasoning that distinguish between valid and invalid inferences using information and digital representation of information. Logic bridges mathematics, computation and philosophy and is the framework through which we assess valid argumentation and discourse. and is the framework My goal in exploring logic and information is to critically examine how formal methods of reasoning and the concept of artificial intelligence is intimately tied racial knowledge and exploitation of labor. Critically engaging with the concept of computational reason, information, and machine intelligence allows us to begin to speculate towards other modes of computation and social relations. I plan to develop a framework to critically engage with existing socio-technical systems multiple levels of abstraction and design alternative modes of computation and technology that are grounded in care, liberation, and justice.

Algorithms of Oppression

Key takeaways

  • Technological Redlining
  • Technological racialization and misogynoir embedded within the architecture of technologies
  • Politics of information classification, access, retrieval, and appraisal
  • Search as a hegemonic method of information access seeks to mimic public institutions but subjects knowledge and information to private interests and market forces

The Digitally Dispossessed

Key Takeaways

  • Racial capitalism and information are linked through value and forms of disavowal. Both digitally and the law of value are premised on the domination of the material by the abstract
  • Information and Value are both tools of measuring difference that enable dispossession
  • Racialization plays the role in the informatics of value of allocating capacity, formal freedom, and liberal personhood

Algorithm = Logic + Control

  • Kowalski's paper makes explicit the connection between logic and internal control of a systems processes that enables the operations of algorithms.
  • I argue that the control of computational, and digital systems finds it's roots in management science, cybernetics, and the labor discipline procedures of Babbage and Taylorism management

The Logic of Information

  • LIS as a discipline can be understood as applied Philosophy of Information. There is a need explicate the implicit Philosophies of information that guide the discipline
  • Information can be understood as a form of poeisis and conceptual design. An information agent is a critical producer of knowledge and information
  • Focus on the producer and logic that guides the production of information not simply the recipient of information
  • Floridi's method of Level of Abstraction

The Eye of the Master

  • material theory of abstraction and labor theory of machine intelligence
  • AI is a product of labor and social relations

Potential Research Questions

  1. Does artificial intelligence reinforce the idea of normative modes of reasoning?
  2. How might we develop a critical approach and radical politics of logic and computation?
  3. What alternative models of computation, reasoning, and organizing information infrastructure exist? How do these alternative models inform our practice as information scholars and critical scholars of technology
  4. How might we begin to understand race as a form of information that is made legible using racialized meaning making systems?
  5. What is the relationship between information and logic? How might a better understanding of information and logic and computational reason guide our interventions in critical AI studies?
